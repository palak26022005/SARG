Advanced Research Module

Master Cancer Research & Molecular Cloning

This module includes hands-on laboratory sessions where participants learn cell culture techniques, such as subculturing, passaging, and cryopreservation of cancer cells. You'll also learn about quality control measures, cell viability assays, and monitoring cell growth and behavior.

Additionally, participants receive training on experimental techniques and assays used in cancer research, such as drug screening, genetic manipulation, and molecular analysis of cancer cells.

Cancer cell culture and molecular biology techniques empower researchers to unravel the complexities of cancer, facilitating the development of novel treatments and improved understanding of the disease.

Learning Outcomes

Skills You Will Master

Upon completion, you will possess advanced research skills in cancer cell culture and molecular biology.

01

Cancer Cell Line Management

Independently maintain, passage, freeze, and revive human cancer cell lines following strict sterile protocols.

02

Molecular Cloning Expertise

Perform complete cloning workflows including restriction digestion, ligation, transformation, and clone screening.

03

Gene Expression Analysis

Extract RNA, synthesize cDNA, and perform RT-PCR to analyze gene expression in cancer cells.

04

Protein Characterization

Extract proteins from cancer cells and analyze using SDS-PAGE and Coomassie blue staining techniques.

05

Drug Cytotoxicity Testing

Design and execute MTT assays to evaluate drug effects on cancer cell proliferation and viability.

06

Bioinformatics Applications

Perform primer designing, promoter analysis, and protein/DNA sequence analysis using computational tools.
